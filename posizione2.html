Welcome to Hotel Pelosi, where our unbeatable location in Lonato Brescia offers you privileged access to a number of exceptional attractions. We are proud to present you some of the places of interest that are easily reachable from our hotel, guaranteeing you an unforgettable stay: Just 10 minutes away, you can immerse yourself in culture and art at the Ugo da Como Museum. This museum houses a large collection of artwork, providing an inspiring experience for fine art enthusiasts. The Civic Ornithological Museum is also just 10 minutes away from the hotel. Here you can explore the fascinating world of birds and discover a variety of species and natural environments. Just 5 minutes away, you will find the renowned Da Giogera restaurant. You will be delighted by a refined and authentic cuisine, where local flavors blend with innovative culinary techniques. If you are looking for a unique gastronomic experience, we suggest you visit Ezio's Trattoria di Pizzeria, just 15 minutes away. Here you can enjoy authentic Italian pizzas and traditional dishes, prepared with fresh, high-quality ingredients. 20 minutes away, you can discover the majestic Rocca di Lonato. This old castle offers a breathtaking view over the city and invites you to immerse yourself in the history and medieval architecture. For those wishing to explore the historic center of Lonato, a pleasant 8-minute walk from the hotel will suffice. Here you can admire the charming historic buildings, stroll through the cozy streets and discover charming boutiques and traditional cafés.

rocca di lonato

The Rocca di Lonato del Garda (or Castello di Lonato) is a fortified building that overlooks the town of Lonato del Garda, in the province of Brescia.

Fornaci romane

It is one of the six kilns of a vast artisan complex for the production of bricks needed in the first phase of the Romanisation of Lake Garda.
Clay, water and wood: three elements necessary for the existence of this craft complex, certainly present in the area of ​​the morainic amphitheater of Garda, elements so important that even in the fourteenth century, in this area, a furnace was still producing bricks.
